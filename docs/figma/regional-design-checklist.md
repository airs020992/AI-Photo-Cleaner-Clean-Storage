# Regional Design Checklist

## Purpose

This app targets overseas utility users across 20 locales. Translation alone is not enough. The same core flow should adapt to regional expectations around privacy, visual density, payment confidence, text length, and RTL behavior.

Use this checklist during Figma design review and before each localized store screenshot export.

## en-US

- Lead with direct value: storage recovered, duplicates found, videos taking space.
- Permission copy should be plain and confidence-building.
- Dashboard must show recoverable storage first.
- Paywall copy should be restrained and transparent.
- Do not over-explain; users expect fast utility.

## zh-CN

- Use clear feature hierarchy and visible before/after results.
- Avoid copy that sounds like fake system cleanup or phone booster language.
- Emphasize preview, confirmation, and recoverable space.
- Keep screenshots dense enough to show utility value.

## es

- Use benefit-forward screenshot copy.
- Highlight visible space recovery and simple one-tap review.
- Annual pricing should feel like value, not pressure.
- Avoid dense legal wording on the primary visual path.

## pt-BR

- Show before/after storage benefit prominently.
- Use warmer success states than Western Europe, while keeping the app professional.
- Make rewarded unlocks easy to understand.
- Annual subscription framing should stress value-for-money.

## ru

- Keep UI efficient and pragmatic.
- Avoid exaggerated trust claims.
- Show concrete file counts and storage numbers.
- Make cancellation and premium state clear.

## ja

- Use compact, precise layouts.
- Microcopy should be calm and exact.
- Emphasize safety before deletion and user control.
- Avoid exaggerated emotional claims.
- Use high polish in spacing and alignment; visible roughness damages trust.

## ko

- Compact, high-efficiency interface.
- Strong emphasis on result clarity and fast task completion.
- Avoid overly decorative visual treatment.
- Keep buttons short and familiar.

## de

- Test long text in buttons, cards, and paywall.
- Permission copy should emphasize privacy and user control.
- Subscription cancellation wording must be visible.
- Avoid high-pressure visual treatment.
- Keep colors restrained and information credible.

## fr

- Privacy and subscription clarity should be visible.
- Avoid aggressive urgency language.
- Use elegant but practical layout rhythm.
- Paywall should not visually trap the user.

## ar

- Full RTL layout is mandatory.
- Mirror navigation, progress direction, media selection flow, and bottom sheet alignment.
- Use larger tap targets for expanded text.
- Keep media examples culturally neutral.
- Avoid layouts that assume left-to-right reading order.

## id

- Onboarding should be short and direct.
- Show free scan result before any paid ask.
- Emphasize storage recovery and simple cleanup.
- Keep screenshots understandable on lower-end devices.

## th

- Text expansion must be tested in cards and buttons.
- Avoid cramped vertical spacing.
- Show concrete storage numbers.
- Rewarded unlocks should be visually explicit.

## vi

- Focus on fast scan, visible results, and free preview.
- Avoid heavy text blocks.
- Use clear category cards and simple confirmation copy.
- Paywall should appear only after scan value is visible.

## tr

- Present value directly.
- Use clear subscription terms and annual discount messaging.
- Avoid fake urgency.
- Keep dashboard scan result prominent.

## it

- Keep the experience polished and straightforward.
- Show photo/video categories clearly.
- Avoid overly technical permission wording.
- Premium should feel like convenience, not coercion.

## el

- Test text length in paywall and dashboard.
- Keep visual hierarchy simple.
- Emphasize preview before deletion.
- Avoid dense legal copy in primary flow.

## pl

- Text expansion needs validation.
- Use practical, result-first copy.
- Make deletion confirmation very clear.
- Keep pricing and cancellation visible.

## nl

- Privacy-first and concise.
- Avoid unnecessary visual decoration.
- Keep subscription copy transparent.
- Dashboard should feel efficient and low-noise.

## sv

- Minimal, calm, privacy-aware.
- Avoid aggressive colors and pressure tactics.
- Emphasize user control and clear cancellation.
- Keep layout sparse enough to feel trustworthy.

## hi

- Test text expansion in dashboard cards and paywall.
- Keep onboarding fast and readable.
- Show free scan result before paywall.
- Ensure buttons remain usable on small screens.
- Use result-first copy: space recovered, items found, safe review.

## Store Screenshot Rules

- Each localized screenshot set should show actual app value, not abstract marketing.
- At least one screenshot must show recoverable storage.
- At least one screenshot must show preview before deletion.
- Paywall screenshots should not be the first screenshot.
- Arabic screenshots must use RTL UI, not LTR UI with Arabic text.
